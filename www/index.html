The Sloan Atlas of Galaxies

by David W. Hogg
Center for Cosmology and Particle Physics, Department of Physics, New York University

The Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) and its follow-on projects SDSS-II and SDSS-III took five-band digital CCD imaging of nearly one quarter of the sky. The Sloan Atlas of Galaxies is an objectively constructed visualization of these data at the positions of the angularly largest galaxies on the sky.

The Sloan Atlas is different from previous efforts in this area in a number of important ways:

  • The data are based on linear, high-quantum-efficiency digital CCD data, not photographic plates. The dynamic range of the data is large, and images of different galaxies can be visualized in a precisely comparable manner.
  • The images are multi-band and can therefore be rendered (in a number of ways) as human-viewable RGB (screen) or CMYK (page) images.
  • The galaxies are selected on objective criteria, themselves acting on quantitative measurements, not subjective ideas of beauty, morphological class, or exceptional properties. Statistical properties of galaxies in the Sloan Atlas sample can be translated into statistical properties of the galaxy population as a whole.

The Sloan Atlas of Galaxies is forthcoming from the Princeton University Press.
